- Impaired lymphocyte development and signaling: defects in T-cell receptor complex, B-cell receptor signaling, and co-stimulatory pathways leading to combined immunodeficiency and antibody deficiencies.
- Dysregulated cytokine and interferon signaling via JAK-STAT and NF-kappa-B pathways causing immune activation defects, autoimmunity, and autoinflammation.
- DNA repair, chromatin remodeling, and telomere maintenance defects resulting in bone marrow failure syndromes, developmental delay, and increased infection susceptibility.
- Inflammasome and autophagy pathway abnormalities contributing to autoinflammatory disorders with systemic inflammation and recurrent fevers.
- Complement system dysregulation associated with atypical hemolytic uremic syndrome, recurrent infections, and immune complex-mediated tissue damage.
- Clinical phenotypes include recurrent bacterial, viral, and fungal infections, lymphoproliferation, cytopenias, inflammatory bowel disease, and syndromic features such as skeletal anomalies and neurodevelopmental delay.
