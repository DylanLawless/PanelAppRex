This gene set focuses on primary immunodeficiencies and monogenic inflammatory bowel diseases presenting with recurrent infections, immune dysregulation, autoimmunity, and inflammatory bowel pathology. Clinical features include combined or selective T- and B-cell deficiencies, hypogammaglobulinemia, neutropenia, lymphadenopathy, hepatosplenomegaly, and susceptibility to viral, bacterial, and fungal pathogens. Syndromic forms may exhibit developmental delay, skeletal abnormalities, and bone marrow failure. Autoimmune cytopenias, enteropathy, and autoinflammatory manifestations are frequent. Complement deficiencies and dysregulated cytokine signaling contribute to disease phenotypes. Early-onset severe infections, failure to thrive, and immune dysregulation syndromes such as hemophagocytic lymphohistiocytosis and autoimmune lymphoproliferative syndrome are represented.
