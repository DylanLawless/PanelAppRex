- Disrupted collagen fibrillogenesis and extracellular matrix organization lead to skeletal dysplasias characterized by abnormal bone growth, epiphyseal enlargement, and platyspondyly.
- Craniofacial anomalies including cleft palate, micrognathia, and Pierre Robin sequence arise from impaired cartilage and connective tissue development.
- Progressive sensorineural hearing loss results from defects in collagen-rich structures of the inner ear.
- Ocular abnormalities such as myopia, cataracts, vitreoretinal degeneration, and retinal detachment reflect compromised vitreous and retinal extracellular matrix.
- Early-onset joint laxity and arthrosis stem from defective cartilage matrix composition and maintenance.
- Vitreoretinopathy with vitreous degeneration and retinal atrophy is linked to altered proteoglycan balance in the extracellular matrix.
